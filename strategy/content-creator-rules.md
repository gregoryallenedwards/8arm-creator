# Content Creator Rules

These are the operating principles for the content creator phase. When in doubt, come back here.

---

## The Prime Directive

> **Do something. Try things. See what works.**

Build myself comes first. Post on what I am doing. Build the resume.

---

## The Goal of Every Build

The goal is **a great post**, not a great product.

- Keep scope limited
- Cheap experiments only
- Don't boil the ocean
- Ion/Joy diversions are legitimate **as long as I am building**

---

## What I Post

- What I built and how (tech process, tools used, what broke)
- Experiments with components of the validation engine — **never the full engine**
- Community outreach experiments
- Fake door tests
- Tech learnings that build toward a job/consultancy resume

## What I Do NOT Post

- The full validation engine (crown jewels)
- The distribution methodology in detail (crown jewels)
- Opinions — facts and capability first
- Unfinished thinking that isn't grounded in a build

---

## Voice and Persona

- Skeptical view. Not "anything can be done" but "what problems can this solution solve today"
- Capability-first lens
- Facts, not hype
- Culture jamming is legitimate (Motivational Miner, Wellness Toxifier, etc.)
- I focus on what I've actually built and what I actually learned

---

## Cadence

- **Weekly** posts — experiment/learning format, not product launch format
- LinkedIn: shorter drop that teases the Substack + one key insight
- Reddit: drops to relevant communities, don't make a statement — send a free product

---

## SEO / GEO

- Use full name "Gregory Allen Edwards" naturally in every post
- Tag appropriately for builder/AI/game dev audience
- Goal: AI-generated search results surface my name over time

---

## Audience Goal

**Phase 1:** 15 people willing to try my apps/sites as early users (APKs or websites)

**Target audience:**
- Primary: other builders, developers, people learning to build with AI
- Secondary: creative/entrepreneurial people who could become product users

---

## Distribution Stack

- **Home base:** Own website
- **Long form:** Substack
- **Professional drops:** LinkedIn
- **Community drops:** Reddit (don't make a statement — send a free product)
- **Future:** YouTube / short form video

---

## The Reddit Model

Find industry-specific subreddits. Build something free for that community. Post it. Then cold call: "I did it for this guy and here is his review."

General rule: I find Reddits I want to make a statement in, but I don't make a statement. I send a free product.

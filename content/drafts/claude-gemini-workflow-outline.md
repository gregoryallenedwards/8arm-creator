# Outline: Maximizing AI Output: My Dual-Subscription Workflow (Claude & Gemini)

## 1. Introduction
- **Opening Note:** Confession—I actually started writing this article with Claude. But after a long day of coding, I ran out of credits and had to switch over to Google's Antigravity to finish it. You have to prepare your workflow for these exact moments, but doing so is a great way to manage costs and ensure you're using your credits on the LLMs that are optimized for specific activities.
- **The Core Problem:** Hitting token limits and session caps during heavy AI-assisted development.
- **The Solution:** Operating with both a $20 Claude subscription and a $20 Google AI (Gemini) subscription.
- **Audience Context:** A brief explanation of how token limits and session credits actually work for both the $20 Claude and $20 Google AI subscriptions.

## 2. The Value Breakdown: Google AI vs. Claude
- **Google AI Subscription Advantages:**
  - Incredible value bundle: Now includes YouTube Premium Lite.
  - Built-in image creation capabilities.
  - *Caveat on Images:* Explored replacing Midjourney with Gemini. While Gemini currently lacks the strict consistency of Midjourney, the Google bundle still maintains a massive price advantage.
- **Claude Subscription Advantages:**
  - Exceptional at UI/UX through Claude Design.
  - The unmatched coding quality of Claude Code Opus for complex logic.

## 3. The Multi-Agent Workflow (Step-by-Step)
- **Phase 1: Prototyping & Design (Claude)**
  - Spend the bulk of session/design credits in Claude.
  - Focus on creating the initial prototype and nailing the design.
  - *Trigger:* Usually run through most of the session credits by the time this phase is done.
- **Phase 2: The Handoff (Git)**
  - Check the initial prototype and all assets into Git to secure the state.
- **Phase 3: Deep Building (Antigravity IDE / Gemini)**
  - Have the Antigravity IDE pick up the repo and start building out the heavier implementation details.
- **Phase 4: Structuring & Mobile (Cowork & Dispatch)**
  - Use **Cowork** to strategically structure the work and keep the project organized.
  - Use **Dispatch** to continue managing the project and executing tasks while away from the computer.
- **Phase 5: The Fallback Loop**
  - When token limits are hit on Cowork: Push everything to Git.
  - Spin up a new Antigravity session to pick up the repo and continue the work uninterrupted.

## 4. Conclusion
- Summary of how bouncing back and forth between the two ecosystems eliminates token bottlenecks and keeps momentum high.
- Final thoughts on the ROI of spending $40/month to unlock a continuous, multi-agent development pipeline.

## The Claim (the through line)

**"I ship commercially successful games with AI-sized teams."**

Every project, post, and application either proves this claim, funds the pursuit of it, or gets cut. Allen is not positioning as "a producer" or "an AI builder" separately. The position is the intersection: 30 years of greenlight and shipping judgment, applied with AI-native production methods. The producer history makes the AI content credible; the AI building makes the producer history current.

## End Goal

Own the AI-native game production position by 2030. The vehicle (own studio, micro-publisher of other people's games, or fractional/consulting portfolio) is deliberately undecided. It will be chosen based on what the market responds to during Phases 0 and 1. All three vehicles are the same business: selling greenlight judgment plus AI-native production.

Financial objective: maximize total income January 2027 through December 2037. Any equity bet must be capable of paying out by ~2033 to count.

## Hard Constraints

- Work rights: US only without sponsorship. UK employment ends with My.Games. The US is the target market.
- Runway: under 12 months of savings after salary ends. Income floor is required by mid-2027.
- Salary ends: garden leave starts end of October 2026; fully unemployed January 1, 2027.
- The traditional games-exec job market has not responded in two years. Returning to a salaried EP/production role is not Plan A and does not get priority effort.

## Decision Rules

1. Every active project must either prove the Claim or advance the income floor. If it does neither, it does not get worked on.
2. At most ONE flagship and TWO side projects active at any time.
3. Side projects must produce a great post within 2 weeks or be put back in the backlog. The flagship is exempt from the 2-week rule.
4. Project statuses in PROJECTS.md are changed only when Allen explicitly says so. Models recommend; Allen decides.
5. New ideas are scored with the Fit Check in PROTOCOL.md. Score below 7: the default answer is no.
6. Joy projects (Ian persona) are legitimate as long as Allen keeps building, but they never displace the flagship and are capped at the side-project limit.
7. The validation engine components are crown jewels. Post about individual components only, never the assembled engine.

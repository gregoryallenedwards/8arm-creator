# Business Strategy

## Priority Order (strict)

1. **Content creator first** — everything else flows from this
2. **Builder job / consultancy / VC credibility** — enabled by demonstrated builds and audience
3. **VC pitch (3A)** or **audience product monetization (3B)**

---

## Phase 1 — Content Creator (NOW)

**Platforms:** LinkedIn + Substack. Home base is own website.

**Operating principles:**
- Don't boil the ocean. Try cheap experiments.
- Scope is intentionally small: **goal is a great POST, not a great product**
- Goal: get 15 early testers willing to try apps or websites (APKs/sites)
- Post what you are building and what you are learning
- Facts not opinions. Capability-first lens. Skeptical view.

**What to post:**
- Components of the validation engine (not the full engine — that's a crown jewel)
- Experiments with community outreach
- Fake door tests
- Tech learnings (useful for job credibility too)
- Automation experiments

**Distribution:**
- Reddit drops → funnel hardcore to Substack + YouTube (model: Ryan McBeth meets Eric Seufert)
- LinkedIn drops (long-form posts: "Here's what I built in 3 hours with these tools")
- Community outreach: find industry subreddits, build something free, cold call with proof

**Brand:** Gregory Allen Edwards / 8arm. Sometimes called "8 arms" (octopus builder metaphor).

## Phase 3A — VC Route: "Amazon of Niches"

**Core thesis:** A repeatable engine to find underserved niches (where big players aren't competing), validate demand cheaply, then distribute a white-label product skinned for that market.

**Validation engine components** (crown jewels — do NOT give away publicly):
- Sentiment crawling (cheaply, ideally manual web scraping without APIs)
- Market size estimation
- Fake door ad testing (next step after sentiment)
- Community outreach (B2B cold calling and community posts)
- Analytics + feedback review to decide on additional content/features

**Distribution:**
- White-label products skinned per niche
- Applies to: digital (video, audio, apps) and eventually physical design
- Future: narrow-cast every conceivable niche the big players ignore

**VC pitch angle:** Show you can start with a small market. Capital is for scaling to more markets.

**Build list (white-label candidates):**
- Match 3 white label
- "Chino" white label
- Gamified apps

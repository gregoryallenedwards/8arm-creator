## What It Is

A repeatable system for finding underserved niches, validating demand cheaply, and distributing a white-label product skinned for that market.

This is the core moat for the VC pitch (Phase 3A). It's also the thing that makes the content creator phase valuable — each post is an experiment with one component.

---

## The Steps

### 1. Sentiment Crawling
- Gauge sentiment around needs from a number of sources
- Ideally: manual web scraping, no API required
- Tools to explore: Firecrawl, manual Reddit/forum reading, Jina AI Reader
- Ask: what are people complaining about? What do they keep asking for?

### 2. Market Size Estimation
- Estimate TAM for the niche
- Look at subreddit size, search volume, App Store category rankings
- Is this big enough to be worth a free app drop?

### 3. First Moment of Interest (Manual)
- Before building anything: can I find organic evidence that people want this?
- Manual search first. Computer-assisted second.

### 4. Fake Door Testing
- Next step after sentiment
- Post something that implies the product exists. Measure clicks/signups.
- Do NOT build until fake door shows real interest.

### 5. Community Outreach
- B2B cold calling and community posts are acceptable
- Build something free for the community, post it, measure response
- Cold call with proof: "I did it for this guy, here's his review"

### 6. Build + Distribute
- White-label product skinned for the market
- Measure: analytics, feedback, retention
- Decide: add features, move on, or kill it

### 7. Feedback Loop
- Review sentiment + analytics + user feedback
- Is there additional content or features that would make this market successful?

- "I ran a fake door test on [subreddit] — here's what happened"
- "Cold outreach to a community: what worked and what didn't"
- "How I estimated market size for a niche app in 2 hours"

**Never post:** the framework as a system, the full methodology, or anything that reveals the repeatable engine.

---

## VC Pitch Angle

Show that you can start with a small market and prove the system works. Capital is for scaling to more markets — not for proving the model.

The "Amazon of niches" thesis: use this method to narrow-cast every conceivable niche where big players aren't competing. Applies to digital (video, audio, apps) and eventually physical design.
